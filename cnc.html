What is CNC...?

CNC - Computer Numerical Control ,in which Machining is based on the computer controls(any controller).

Why Number comes in play ?

This is a number based control where each number has the seperate Value. Each number is assigned to separate function and work. Which we will be seeing it in the upcoming steps.

Step 1: What Is Machining

Machining as simple can be said as a process of removal of the material form the work piece to get the desirable and needed shape. The Shape and Size of the output depends on the MOVEMENT OF THE TOOL or JOB and not because of the ROTATION.

The Machining process may be broadly classified as follows:

  • Lathe machining-(JOB rotate and TOOL move)
  • Mill Machining-(JOB move and TOOL rotate)
  • Drill Machining-(JOB stationary and TOOL rotate)


You may see the you tube videos for the good understanding about the working of the various Machining process.



NOTE:

  • In this JOB means the material which you want to make to required shape and size.
  • "TOOL is simply a tool"


As simple to understand : In your Home , Knife is a tool and vegetables or bread is job , (TOOL knife) which deforms the shape of (JOB vegetable )as required by (YOU the MACHINE).

Step 2: CNC Classification

The CNC machine can be basically classified into two types as follows :

CNC Lathe Machine :

Horizontal Operations
JOB will rotate and TOOL will move.
Can be called as TURNING CENTRE.

CNC Milling Machine :

Vertical Operations.
JOB will move and TOOL will rotate..
Can be called as MACHINING CENTRE

Step 3: CNC FUNCTIONS

CNC coding are basically in the Machine Level languages, there is a universal set of codes depending on the function and Operation the CNC does.



Function of the CNC machine can be classified broadly as follows:

  • AUXILIARY FUNCTION(M-CODE)
  • PREPARATORY OR MAIN FUNCTION(G-CODE)
  • SPINDLE SPEED FUNCTION(S-CODE)
  • FEED FUNCTION(F-CODE)
  • TOOL FUNCTION(T-CODE)


NOTE:

  • Here I am focusing on the Lathe type CNC machines.
  • The code may change according to the CNC machines, some codes may be similar. So It is advised to see the Manual of the CNC manufacturer before proceeding any further.
  • Also it is required to know only very few CNC codes for understanding purpose only.
  • Most important codes may be printed on the CNC machine itself.


Step 4: AUXILIARY FUNCTION-(M CODE)

As the name indicates, the auxiliary function is subordinate actions in the CNC machine. It is also called as the M-CODE



Some of the examples of this function are:

  • Spindle ON or OFF
  • Door Opening
  • Coolent Operation


Some of the Common M codes in all the CNC machine are as follows :

  • M03- Spindle ON clockwise
  • M04-Spindle ON counter clockwise
  • M05- spindle OFF
